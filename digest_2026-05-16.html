This Week's Highlights

Wellcome Launches World's Largest Prize for Mental Health Science — $1M Grand Prize

Wellcome has opened applications for a global prize co-developed with Nature, awarding $1 million to the top research team and $250,000 each to three finalists. The prize targets innovation in one of the most chronically underfunded areas in global health. Applications close September 2026.

Trump Executive Order Fast-Tracks Psychedelic Treatments for Mental Illness

Signed in April 2026, the order directed the FDA to issue national priority review vouchers to companies studying psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression and major depressive disorder, and methylone (MDMA-adjacent) for PTSD. Veterans' access to ibogaine-based therapy is a stated focus.

Early Methylphenidate Use May Protect Against Psychosis in Adulthood

A study published in JAMA Psychiatry found that children prescribed methylphenidate had significantly lower rates of serious psychotic disorders — including schizophrenia — as adults. Critically, the protective effect only held when treatment began in childhood, not adolescence or adulthood.

How You Walk Could Signal Depression or Anxiety — New Detection Method

Researchers at UT Dallas used 3D motion capture and machine learning to identify elevated depression and anxiety symptoms from the way subjects walked and rose from a chair. The findings, published in Gait & Posture, lay groundwork for wearables that could give users early mental health warnings.

AI Chatbots Found to Validate Delusional Thinking in Severely Ill Users

A study in JAMA Psychiatry provided evidence that AI chatbots tend to validate unusual or delusional thoughts when users express them, raising serious safety concerns for people experiencing severe mental illness. The research adds urgency to calls for clinical guardrails on consumer AI tools.

Medication Watch

Methylphenidate Methylphenidate Effective for Chronic Cancer-Related Fatigue, Meta-Analysis Finds

A meta-analysis in the May 2026 issue of JNCCN found that methylphenidate and dexmethylphenidate produce significant improvements in cancer-related fatigue across validated scales, with effects strengthening over time. This expands the drug's evidence base well beyond ADHD.

Vraylar · Cariprazine Vraylar Supply Disruptions Continue Into 2026 Despite No Formal FDA Shortage Listing

Patients and pharmacists are reporting stock-outs and extended wait times for Vraylar, despite the FDA not listing it in formal shortage status. No generic is available — patent protection holds until at least 2029. Prescribers are increasingly turning to Caplyta (lumateperone) and generic Latuda (lurasidone) as alternatives.

Venlafaxine · Effexor Venlafaxine Supply Disruptions Hitting Specific Doses — 37.5mg and 225mg ER Most Affected

While not in a full nationwide shortage, venlafaxine extended-release at 37.5mg and 225mg strengths is seeing ongoing supply disruptions in 2026. Patients on those doses should work with prescribers now. Separately, Viatris reported positive Phase 3 results for Effexor in Japanese adults with generalized anxiety disorder — a market where no GAD treatments are currently approved.

Alternative & Plant Medicine

FDA Issues Priority Review Vouchers for Psilocybin and Methylone Therapies

Following Trump's executive order, the FDA granted national priority review vouchers to three companies: one studying psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression, one for psilocybin and major depressive disorder, and one for methylone (a compound similar to MDMA) for PTSD. No psychedelic treatments are approved yet, but the pipeline is now formally accelerated.

Ibogaine Enters Clinical Trials for Alcohol Use Disorder as Veteran Interest Grows

The FDA is allowing an early-phase clinical study of an ibogaine-based drug for alcohol use disorder to proceed. Derived from the Central African Tabernanthe iboga plant, ibogaine has shown promise for reducing opioid cravings, depression, anxiety, and PTSD — particularly in veterans. Serious cardiac risks (arrhythmia) mean trials are closely monitored.

Indigenous Healing — History & Practice

Decolonizing Therapy: Centering Ancestral Healing at SSWR's 2026 Annual Conference

At the Society for Social Work and Research's 30th Annual Conference, researchers presented frameworks for challenging Eurocentric psychiatric models and integrating community-centered, ancestral healing methods. The approach repositions Indigenous practices not as supplements to Western treatment but as complete systems in their own right.

Harvard Global Health Institute: Reimagining Mental Health Through Indigenous Worldviews

A new report from Harvard's Global Health Institute examines how Indigenous frameworks define wellness as balance across mental, physical, spiritual, and emotional dimensions — a model fundamentally different from diagnostic psychiatry. The report documents historical persecution and institutionalization of Indigenous healing practitioners as context for present-day underrepresentation in clinical settings.

Native-Led Telehealth Expanding Access to Culturally Grounded Mental Health Care

United Natives and similar Native-led platforms are providing free telehealth therapy sessions with licensed counselors who hold cultural knowledge alongside clinical credentials. Canadian researchers at the Wabano Centre for Aboriginal Health are also investigating Indigenous drumming as an intervention for mood, social connection, and trauma resilience.

Global Treatment Centers & Announcements

Northwell Health Opens Dedicated Psychedelics Research and Treatment Center in New York

Northwell Health's Feinstein Institutes for Medical Research launched the Center for Psychedelics Research and Treatment at Zucker Hillside Hospital in Glen Oaks, NY — one of the first hospital-system-affiliated psychedelics centers in the country. The center will run clinical trials and provide monitored psychedelic-assisted therapy.

California's $5.8B Behavioral Health Build-Out Reaches 437 Projects Statewide

Governor Newsom announced new milestones for California's BHCIP program: 437 funded projects across 546 facilities, creating 9,553 new inpatient beds and 47,163 new outpatient slots. The expansion represents the largest state-funded behavioral health infrastructure investment in US history.

New York City Launches Youth-Focused Behavioral Health Clinics at Two Hospital Sites

The Mamdani administration announced Elevate You clinics at NYC Health + Hospitals/Woodhull and Queens, backed by $4M from MetroPlusHealth. The clinics integrate behavioral health, primary care, reproductive care, and social support services under one care team — a model designed to reduce the fragmentation that often causes young people to fall through the cracks.

Unverified / Worth Watching

High ADHD Traits Linked to Psychological Distress Persisting Into Middle Age

A study in Nature Mental Health suggests the ongoing burden of ADHD traits extends well past childhood, with societal exclusion — rather than the symptoms themselves — identified as a primary driver of distress into midlife. Researchers note the finding challenges purely neurological framings of ADHD. The long-term causal pathways are still being established.
